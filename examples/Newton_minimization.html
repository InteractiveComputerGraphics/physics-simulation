Newton's method for minimization

Newton's method can be used to find the minimum of a function $f(x)$. At a minimum the derivative vanishes, $f'(x^*) = 0$, so minimization reduces to finding the root of $f'(x)$.

At each iterate $x_n$ a quadratic (second-order Taylor) approximation is formed:

$$q(x) = f(x_n) + f'(x_n)(x-x_n) + \frac{1}{2}f''(x_n)(x-x_n)^2$$

The next iterate $x_{n+1}$ is the minimizer of $q(x)$, giving the update rule:

$$\begin{equation*} x_{n+1} = x_{n} - \frac{f'(x_n)}{f''(x_n)} \end{equation*}$$

The green curves show the quadratic approximation at each step. Provided $f''(x_n) > 0$ and $x_0$ is close enough to a local minimum, the method converges rapidly.
